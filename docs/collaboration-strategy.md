# Collaboration Strategy

The goal is not to ask for funding directly. The goal is to make the project legible enough that serious people can understand where they could help.

## Positioning

Lead with:

> I am building an early concept and prototype direction for SYMBORG, a cognitive front-end layer for human-AI interaction.

Avoid:

> I am building the next Neuralink.

Better framing:

> Neural interfaces and AI systems are improving quickly, but there is still a missing interaction layer: how useful information returns to human attention without overloading or replacing the person.

## Desired Signals

The public launch should signal:

- Serious product thinking
- Ethical restraint
- Awareness of BCI limits
- Assistive communication focus
- Strong visual and narrative design
- Openness to technical critique
- Readiness to prototype

## Who to Attract

Priority audiences:

- HCI researchers
- Neurotechnology ethicists
- Assistive communication builders
- Wearable AI engineers
- Accessibility founders
- Product designers working on attention-sensitive tools
- Investors or operators interested in assistive technology and human-AI interfaces

## What to Ask For

Use high-signal asks:

- "I would value critique from people working on HCI, BCI, assistive communication, and wearable AI."
- "I am looking for collaborators to help turn the first contextual whisper demo into a working prototype."
- "I am especially interested in feedback on evaluation metrics: usefulness, interruption cost, agency, and accessibility."
- "If you work on constrained-input communication or neurotechnology ethics, I would be glad to compare notes."

Avoid low-signal asks:

- "I need funding."
- "Who can connect me to Elon?"
- "This will change humanity."
- "Looking for investors ASAP."

## Credibility Rules

1. Do not overclaim technical maturity.
2. Do not imply affiliation with companies or people.
3. Do not claim medical benefit.
4. Make the first prototype small and testable.
5. Frame the project as a serious interface problem, not a fantasy device.
6. Let the repo, visuals, and roadmap do the signaling.

## Outreach Sequence

1. Publish the repository.
2. Share a LinkedIn post with the hero image and architecture summary.
3. Share an X thread with the core thesis and visuals.
4. Send targeted messages to 10-20 relevant people, not mass spam.
5. Ask for critique, not money.
6. Build the first demo within 2-4 weeks.
7. Share a short video of the demo.

## Private Message Template

```text
Hi [Name],

I am developing SYMBORG, an early-stage concept for a cognitive front-end layer between AI retrieval systems and human attention.

The core question is: if AI can retrieve relevant context in real time, how should that information return to a person without overloading attention or replacing agency?

I am starting with a non-invasive contextual whisper prototype and a more serious assistive communication demo direction for constrained-input users.

I would value your critique, especially on [their domain: HCI / assistive communication / BCI / ethics].

Repo: [link]
```

## What Success Looks Like

Short-term:

- 5-10 serious comments from relevant builders or researchers
- 1-3 conversations with people in HCI, assistive tech, or wearable AI
- A clearer first prototype spec

Medium-term:

- Working contextual whisper demo
- Two-minute demo video
- Better whitepaper with citations
- Assistive communication prototype scenario

Long-term:

- Research collaboration
- Grant-compatible assistive technology direction
- Pilot with accessibility or communication experts
